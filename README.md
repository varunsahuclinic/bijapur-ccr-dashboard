# Bijapur Child Climate Resilience Dashboard (CCR)

**Open Source Digital Public Good**  
**Submitted to UNICEF Venture Fund – Climate Ventures x Health Call (April 2026)**

An interactive, zero-cost, open-source dashboard that aggregates **public data** to provide hyper-local climate-health insights and early warnings specifically for children in **Bijapur district, Chhattisgarh**.

Live Dashboard: **[https://bijapur-ccr-dashboard-ns8aokbwq6ja6ujyos4ard.streamlit.app/](https://bijapur-ccr-dashboard-ns8aokbwq6ja6ujyos4ard.streamlit.app/)**

---

### 🎯 Problem
Bijapur (Bastar region) is one of the most climate-vulnerable districts in India. Children here face:
- Extreme heatwaves
- Erratic monsoons leading to floods & droughts
- High burden of vector-borne diseases (Malaria & Dengue)
- Rising air pollution and respiratory issues
- Malnutrition amplified by climate stress

---

### 🛠️ Solution
The **Bijapur CCR Dashboard** uses only **publicly available data** (Open-Meteo, historical climate records) and simple ML models to deliver:
- Real-time weather monitoring
- 7-day ML-based risk prediction for Malaria & Heat Stress
- Interactive vulnerability map of Bijapur
- **NPCCHH-aligned Early Warnings & Health Advisories** for frontline workers (ASHA, ANM, Anganwadi)

### 🚨 Early Warnings & Health Advisories (Based on NPCCHH Guidelines)

**Heatwave Advisory (NPCCHH / MoHFW Heat Action Plan)**
- Avoid outdoor activities for children between 12 PM – 4 PM
- Ensure frequent hydration with ORS / lemon water (avoid sugary drinks)
- Look for signs of heat exhaustion: dizziness, nausea, rapid breathing
- Keep children in shaded/cool areas; use wet cloth/sponging
- Schools/Anganwadis: Schedule outdoor activities before 11 AM or after 4 PM

**Malaria & Vector-Borne Diseases (Monsoon/High Rainfall)**
- Clear stagnant water around homes, schools & Anganwadi centers
- Use insecticide-treated mosquito nets (especially for children)
- Wear full-sleeve clothes during evening hours
- Seek immediate medical care if child has fever with chills

**Air Quality & Respiratory Health (NPCCHH Air Pollution Guidelines)**
- Stay indoors when AQI is Poor/Very Poor
- Use wet mopping and avoid burning waste
- Children with asthma or respiratory issues should wear masks outdoors
- Monitor symptoms: persistent cough, difficulty breathing

**Flood / Heavy Rain Advisory**
- Store clean drinking water and medicines
- Avoid playing near water bodies
- Boil drinking water to prevent water-borne diseases (diarrhea, cholera)
- Identify safe elevated places in the village

**General Child Protection Tips (NPCCHH)**
- Prioritize nutrition – climate stress increases malnutrition risk
- Maintain vaccination schedule
- Support mental health – extreme weather can cause anxiety in children
